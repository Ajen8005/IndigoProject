Stat Bonuses

  • Faithless characters remove 2 from their total Order (negatives are allowed).

  • Faithless characters add 2 total across any stats of their choice (either 1 in one, and 1 in another, or 2 in a single stat).

  • Faithless characters automatically unlock the Brutality skill.

Abilities

  • Faithless characters can resist being downed (both in and out of combat) by attempting a D20 role. If the result was lower than their current Order, the character stays able to act, even if it’s health is negative. (This must be rolled again each time they may be knocked down.).

  • Faithless may reroll their resist roll one time, when resisting being influenced.

  • if a Faithless character becomes mutated, they may roll a D20. If the result is 15 or above, they resist the negative aspects of such mutation. This can only be attempted once per in-game day..

  • Faithless gain a +2 bonus to all rolls involved with using a knife of any sort, for any reason.

  • Faithless cannot be Demoralized.

Starting Gear

  • a Faithless character begins with a book on simple subjects of their choice. A kitchen knife, casual modern hooded clothing, service-less mobile phone, studded gloves, and a picture of people they have met.

  • a Faithless character can opt to choose the starting equipment of a character in their party instead.

Stat Bonuses

  • Guardians start with 2 bonus Max HP, and 2 bonus Compassion.

  • Guardians start with a bonus + 1 to Hit Prevention..

Abilities

  • Guardians get a + 2 bonus to any rolls where they protect another character (only directly and immediately. Opening a chest with an antidote does not protect someone poisoned in the room.)

  • whenever an ally is downed within arm’s reach of a Guardian, the Guardian may attempt a D20 roll. If the result was above 15, the Guardian prevents the ally from being downed, and the ally’s HP is forcibly placed at 1 (even if the attack initiating this ability would have sent them into the negatives).

  • whenever a Guardian is dealt damage, automatically prevent 1 of that incoming damage.

  • Guardians gain a + 1 to Teamwork rolls, even if Teamwork is not unlocked.

Starting Gear

  • a Guardian begins with a small bag of their choice: empty bookbag, purse, or tactical vest; casual slim clothing, empty lanyard, and wallet with $20 old-world cash in it.

  • Guardians can also choose a household item that could fit within their pocket.

Stat Bonuses

  • Fledged characters gain 1 bonus Compassion.

  • Fledged characters may choose to exchange points of Aggression for Compassion, up to a maximum of 3 points.

Abilities

  • when an ally is dealt damage within vision of a Fledged character, the Fledged character may take 1 of the damage dealt instead.

  • all attempts at healing are double as effective if a Fledged is healing a different character.

  • enemies dealing damage to Fledged characters receive 1 damage as well.

Starting Gear

  • Fledged characters begin with a moderate topical ointment, modern loose clothing, a bottle of water, and a small plushie in the shape of your choice.

Stat Bonuses

  • Bullies gain 2 bonus Aggression.

  • Bullies may select a bonus Aggression skill and unlock it.

Abilities

  • Bullies gain +1 in all their rolls during combat.

  • Bullies deal +2 damage when dealing damage with their fists (gloves, etc. count.)

  • Bullies choose a party member. This Bully adds +2 to all of their Aggression skills when used within arm’s reach of this party member.

  • When Bullies down an opponent, they may roll a D20. If the number is lower than their Speed, they may finish off the opponent instead.

Starting Gear

  • Bullies start with short clothing, a wallet with $40 old-world cash, a sports drink, a cigarette, and a ring belonging to a long past family member.

Stat Bonuses

  • Hard Knocks add half of their speed to their Hit Prevention, but cannot wear Heavy Gear.

  • Hard Knocks automatically unlock Athletics.

  • Faithless characters automatically unlock the Brutality skill.

Abilities

  • Hard Knocks can use either their Speed stat or Aggression stat to add to Athletics rolls.

  • Hard Knocks can ignore any negative side effects of their starting Gene Splicing.

  • Characters do not associate Hard Knocks to their Affiliation in any negative circumstance (ex. character hates The Laboratory and player character’s affiliation is Laboratory. Character will not despise the player character for their affiliation with the Lab.)

  • Gain a bonus +2 to rolls when using non-equipment random objects as weapons.

  • Hard Knocks cannot be Wounded.

Starting Gear

  • Hard Knocks begin with short ugly ragged clothing, a broken golf-club, and a lock pick. They also begin unbathed for weeks.

Stat Bonuses

  • Students gain a bonus 2 stats in Studiousness.

  • Students can exchange up to 2 stats in one stat, for 2 in another (one time.)

  • Students lose 2 Max HP.

Abilities

  • Students automatically unlock Copying.

  • Students gain a bonus + 1 to any rolls involving these skills: Crafting, Teamwork, Innocence, Deceit (only applies if the skill is unlocked).

  • Students can remove a recently (within the last in-game hour) applied genetic mutation from themselves or an ally (once per in-game day.)

Starting Gear

  • Students begin with assigned school clothing, a textbook on the topic of their choice, $10 of old-world cash in their pocket, a pencil, and a functioning, but inaccurate watch.

Stat Bonuses

  • Leaders gain + 1 Order.

  • Leaders can sacrifice 1 stat point from all other stats, to add 4 to Order.

  • When creating their character, Leaders roll a D6. If the result was 1, add 1 point to Aggression. If 2, add 1 point to Order, and so on. 6 Does not provide a point.

Abilities

  • Allies within earshot of a Leader deal 1 additional damage each attack.

  • Allies who become demoralized can attempt to roll a D20 for the main action on their turn, and if the result was lower than their Leader’s Order stat, they recover immediately (they are no longer demoralized).

  • Leaders automatically unlock Commanding.

Starting Gear

  • Leaders begin with formal wear clothes, a hat of their choice, a functioning mobile phone (no current contacts, or connection to internet,) the ability to speak two other languages of choice, and a functioning golden stopwatch.
